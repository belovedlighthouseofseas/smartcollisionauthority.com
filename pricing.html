Pricing Factors

What Affects the Final Cost.

📐
Damage Size

Larger damage areas require more material, more labor, and more time. A small scuff costs less than a full bumper split.

🎨
Paint Type

Standard solid colors are simpler to match. Metallic, pearl, and two-stage paints take more material and technique to blend cleanly.

🔢
Panel Count

Damage that spans more than one panel increases the job scope. Blending adjacent panels takes additional time and material.

⚙️
Repair Type

Surface correction, spot paint, full refinish, and PDR each have different labor and material requirements that affect final cost.

🕐
On-Site Time

More complex repairs take more time on-site. Time is factored into the final price rather than charged as a separate rate.

🚗
Vehicle Type

Larger vehicles, trucks, and SUVs often have larger panels, which can push the repair into a higher range for the same damage type.
